<h2>In Vital’s second lecture Dr Joe Tafur blew minds with a clinical overview of shamanic plant medicine healing. It included his staggering current research into conditions possibly related to epigentics that range from PTSD to cancer.</h2>

He’s the author of ayahuasca blockbuster<a href="https://www.amazon.co.uk/Fellowship-River-Exploration-Traditional-Amazonian/dp/0998609501" target="_blank"><em>The Fellowship of the River</em></a><em> </em>(‘with introduction by Gabor Mat´é’) plus the<em> </em>co-founder alongside Amaringo of <a href="https://nihuerao.com" target="_blank">Nihue Rao</a> healing centre near Iquitos, Peru. </p>

<h2><span class="sqsrte-text-color--custom" style="color: #706E6E"><strong>Saving lost souls for three million years. Now, with MDMA</strong></span></h2>
</div>
<figure style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="https://www.anturiongallery.com/graciela-arias-salazar/"><img decoding="async" src="https://newpsychonaut.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/La-Virgen-del-Capinuri-.avif?format=original" alt="  Graciela Arias Salazar, ‘La Virgen del Capinurí  out of    @centro_selva_arte_y_ciencia    via    Antricion    gallery in Zurich  "></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Graciela Arias Salazar, ‘La Virgen del Capinurí  out of    @centro_selva_arte_y_ciencia    via    Antricion    gallery in Zurich</figcaption></figure>

<h2>Dr Tafur saw ayahuasca’s positive effect on beleagured veterans. </h2>
<p>He took this as a validation of his own conversion to plant medicine after ayahuasca was instrumental in his recovery from a depressive episode.</p>
<p>He told Vital students that transcendent unconditional love, of the kind received in a sacred ceremony, has a positive effect on psychoneuroimmunology (PNI) just like family love (the good enough kind). </p>
<p>It can be transmitted via ‘limbic resonance’ – a theoretical term for hormonal interaction between one or more people. With its support our subconscious processes difficult emotions more efficiently. The healing effect moves from the psyche (P) through the nerous system (N) into the immune system (I), bolstering the body’s own intuitive ‘inner healer’. </p>
<p>The importance of empathy has recently been <a href="https://www.physiciansweekly.com/empathy-the-best-medicine-how-doctors-can-help-patients-remember-more-information-and-make-better-informed-decisions" target="_blank">recognised</a> within western medical treatment. But we are all understanding that professional clinicians can only give so much of themselves.</p>
<p>However. Try instead exotic ceremonies, remarkable locations, skilled practicioners, devout participants, and zealous dedication in the form of the ‘only was out is through’ strategy of taking high-strength ancient jungle acid five nights in a row – and you have the missing element required to treat a range of psychoneuroimmunologically-related conditions currently frustrating doctors and destroying families. </p>
<p>The spiritual sector, to its eternal credit, provides the social role of offering salvation to those mired in confusion, or paralysed by ethical quagmire. It can provide rare complex moral reconciliation, of the kind that PTSD treatment benefits from enormously. Where though does MDMA come in? It’s an ‘empathogen’ as opposed to a psychedelic.</p>
<p>Nonetheless the ‘love drug’ too can augment some characteristics of psychotherapy just like psychedelics and traditional healing ceremonies. Not only does MDMA increase the level of limbic resonance between doctor and patient, it’s also been <a href="https://www.hopkinsmedicine.org/news/newsroom/news-releases/psychedelic-drug-mdma-may-reawaken-critical-period-in-brain-to-help-treat-ptsd" target="_blank">shown to activate areas of the brain used during childhood</a> to ingrain healthy social behaviour patterns.</p>
<p>Besides, “The <a href="https://mdmaptsd.org">MAPS PTSD programme</a> going up for FDA-approval has a mystical element,” sa Dr Tafur, responding to my disbelief that western psychotherapy can rapidly replicate the awe of ayahuasca, “in my experience the clinical sector is increasingly intertested in ceremony. There are some really open-hearted therapists at MAPS,” he expands, referencing the completely accepting nature of spiritual fulfilment… historically known as ecstasy.</p>

<h2><span class="sqsrte-text-color--custom" style="color: #B65575"><strong>Psychedelic therapy is an art first and a science second</strong></span></h2>
</div>
<figure style="width: 850px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" src="https://newpsychonaut.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Anderson-Debernardi-Visionary-Art-ayahuasqueroshealing_1024x1024.avif?format=original" alt="  Anderson Debernardi, ‘Ayahuasqueros Healing’ available    here   "><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Anderson Debernardi, ‘Ayahuasqueros Healing’ available    here</figcaption></figure>

<h2>“It’s living in the jungle for four months, eating right,” says Tafur, “You can’t read it and write it.”</h2>
<p>You can train to be a shaman with Dr Joe! But there’s what would be considered, in this modern world, a catch: you actually have to go and do it. </p>
<p>Even for psychotherapits practicing MDMA therapy at MAPS, Dr Tafur points out, “There’s no running away when you’re in there with people who have these issues for eight hours.”</p>
<p>Whether delivering the icaros in that delightful yet dread-laden way, or deftly reaching out with your <a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4316402/" target="_blank">neuroceptive aura</a>, Dr Tafur is keen to stress, “this is an art.”</p>
<p>A powerful combination of the sacred, the empathic, and experience in healing epigentic-related conditions is central to his hypothesis (see Medical below). It’s why the clincial sector is fascinated; even in <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?index=9&amp;list=PLHkFG2EjLJZFuv59kEov9TWSnsVc2R2qc&amp;t=395s&amp;v=Si-jQeWSDKc" target="_blank">this 1950s archive footage</a> a researcher asks his test subject “how does your soul feel right now?” Plus it’s also why ravers aren’t cured of mental health issues after a big weekend – context and other important characteristics are key to the drug experience having a self-healing element. The spiritual factor prompts an ever deeper form of self-healing when combined with the therapeutic. “Current medical science cannot match the transpersonal, or the moving,” says Dr Tafur. </p>
<p>‘Psyche’ in classical greek means ‘soul’. Dr Tafur explained that indigenous perspectives consider spirituality and healing to be one and the same. Music, prayer, ceremony, connection and affirmation: all augment the ‘spiritual experience’ that research shows is key to healing with psychedelics. </p>

<h2><span class="sqsrte-text-color--custom" style="color: #4682B4"><strong>Songs in the key of life: energy and entropy</strong></span></h2>
</div>
<figure style="width: 1392px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=75V4ClJZME4"><img decoding="async" src="https://newpsychonaut.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/fantastic-guest-Irene-Lopez-Leon-1.avif?format=original" alt="  Irene Lopez, ‘Fantastic Guest’    works available via her site   "></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Irene Lopez, ‘Fantastic Guest’    works available via her site</figcaption></figure>
<div class="sqs-html-content" data-sqsp-text-block-content>
<h2>Mystic healing is far from an established vocation in the West. And the spectre of <a href="https://daily.jstor.org/the-colonization-of-the-ayahuasca-experience/" target="_blank">cultural appropriation</a> casts a deep shadow. </h2>
<p>The western neoshamanism AKA <a href="https://www.shamanism.org/fssinfo/index.html" target="_blank">core shamanism</a> movement has gone through its highs and lows since <a href="https://ayahuasca-timeline.kahpi.net" target="_blank">explorers came across ayahuasca</a> in the 1800s. </p>
<p>But there’s a gulf between Instagram ‘shamans’ and the type of grassroots work conducted for example in communities affected by the opioid crisis, where very well-intentioned and ultimately effective healers face risks of incarceration and rupturing their own community relations.</p>
<p>How then can mystical healing’s beneficial elements be adopted by western health and wellbeing practicioners?</p>
<p>Apprenticing under an authentic expert would likely involve the path taken by, for example, Reiki healers who are also encouraged to search and wait for the correct master, and may need to prove their dedication upon finding them.</p>
<p>“It’s about real people and some real conenction that you might have,” says Dr Tafur, “What opportunities do you have around you? Where are you? Is there somebody in your area where you can explore that?”</p>
<p>There are nonetheless fundamentals to the approach that can be considered. MAPS training assets for example refer to the importance of the therapist exuding ‘loving presence’. And somatic experiencing (which Dr Tafur recommends) coaches are trained to cultivate a ‘<a href="https://khironclinics.com/blog/polyvagal-theory-neuroception-the-fundament-of-feeling/" target="_blank">neuroceptive aura</a>’ radiating a sense of safety to encourage ‘<a href="https://www.theguardian.com/science/2021/aug/15/the-hidden-sense-shaping-your-wellbeing-interoception" target="_blank">interoception</a>’ a constructive dialogue between the body, emotions, and conscious thought. </p>
<p>“We’re trying to help somebody, and using our faculties as human beings in order to do that,” is what Dr Tafur distills the healing process down to. During his lecture he was hardly dismissive of authentic western efforts to replicate the process – but it’s essential that these are conducted with an ‘open heart’.</p>
<p>Singing icaros-type songs with substituted words in a shared language is not misguided… as long as it creates the required atmosphere for a mystical healing window to open. Sincerity of feeling is essential, but localised interpretations may be actually more effective than exotic traditional ones.</p>
<p>The most graceful nuances of ayahuasca are said to be ontologically lost on outsiders. Occultist <a href="https://magick.me" target="_blank">Jason Louv</a> points out that eastern spiritual systems like buddhism are guides to living well in different cultures. In response to social media claims that ‘Putin should take ayahuasca’ to end the Ukraine conflict, Shaur-trained shaman and former financier John Perkins recently pointed out that the experience is traditionally believed to make <a href="https://johnperkins.org/blog/putin-and-ayahuasca" target="_blank">fiercer warriors</a> to highlight how we can’t overlap a different approach entirely onto our own worldview. Tactics specific to western society may have more impact on local patients, if conducted with the same levels of intention, craft and sensitivity. </p>
<p>The “energy” to quote Dr Tafur directly is what is key – its purity of purpose and its intensity. Icaros have an improvised element and he himself will substitute spanish or english words if required. Nonetheless the language has a vocabulary that is ideal to express the ayahuasca experience, as does the rhythm – but this itself features aspects that might well go over the heads of anyone who can’t claim lineage in the local society and culture. </p>
<p>Equally any expert will tell you that it is best to learn an established system before beginning to develop your own. Dr Tafur feels that his traditional Shipibo education gives his practice, “A strong base. But I’m interested in singing for patients undergoing ketamine therapy for example. So there’s room to mix it up. But there must be a reason why. Is it because it’s better for your patients or just because you don’t like tradition? Learning something well, then applying it elsewhere is more powerful than just coming up with whatever.”</p>
<p>Europeans have been feverishly dreaming up a shamanic culture using both fact and myth for the past few centuries, resulting in for example Wicca. They have also looked to the future in the form of ‘<a href="https://screenrant.com/doom-patrol-grant-morrison-occult-philosophy-chaos-magic/" target="_blank">chaos magic</a>’, a method of creating personal spiritual systems based on esoteric techniques passed down the millennia. Even a traditional ‘christian mysticism’ featuring, for example alchemy is in vogue now thanks to writer Damien Echols. </p>
<p>All this will still be a massive culture shock for the average agnostic westerner seeking alternative healthcare, especially hipsters raised on Richard Dawkins (who also has his place). But often the psychedelically-active patient will have become curious about these areas allowing for fundamentals to be touched upon at least. In 2015’s <em>Transcendent Mind</em> (see bottom of page) published by the famously orthodox American Psychological Association, Drs Imants Baruss and Julia Mossbridge present serious, rubber-stamped research that pairs ancestor worship with the collective unconscious and (I paraphrase) ‘that way you know your parents are about to call just before the phone rings.’ Refer to that last bit and riff from there…</p>
<p>To end. Experts like Dr Ido Cohen point out that the shamanic path is a calling as opposed to a blessing. Like other (supposedly) rewarding life paths, it comes with its own trials and sacrifices. Dr Tafur says, “if this makes you feel special, like you’re right and others are wrong, it’s not working. It’s suppsoed to make you feel part of something, working together.”</p>

<h2><span class="sqsrte-text-color--custom" style="color: #26AE3C">Spiritual</span><span class="sqsrte-text-color--custom" style="color: #1E931E"> </span><span class="sqsrte-text-color--custom" style="color: #26AE3C">healing could help cure epigentic diseases and transform treatment</span></h2>

<figure style="width: 1280px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="https://pablo-amaringo.pixels.com"><img decoding="async" src="https://newpsychonaut.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/dna-1280x720-1.avif?format=original" alt="  Plant medicine fan favourite    Pablo Amringo   "></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Plant medicine fan favourite    Pablo Amringo</figcaption></figure>

<h2>“Ultimately we’re reopening metaphysics. The research is in review now and we’re looking forward to sharing it with the world,” says Dr Tafur.</h2>
<p><a href="https://www.modernspirit.org" target="_blank">Modern Spirit</a>, Dr Tafur’s non-profit org has collected genetic samples from some of the 107 patients in MAPS’ 12-month MDMA therapy trial (see above) where a stunning 68% of patients had statistically recovered from chronic, treatment resistant PTSD after three treatments. They’d been suffering for an average of 17.8 years.</p>
<p>“To a doctor, the sacred isn’t important. But to close your mind is a hiding place. People need to see something. Mental health? It’s competitive. But cancer is largely epigenetic and that’s one of the fields they’re saying they want to put more energy and understanding into.” </p>
<p>The research could also validate other holistic practices ranging from somatic experiencing to reiki.</p>
<p>“Ultimately we’re reopening metaphysics. The research is in review now and we’re looking forward to sharing it with the world,” he says.</p>
<p>He recounts a pivotal moment in his shamanic career, when ayauasca visions insisted that a penitent’s disease was “on” as opposed to “in” her genes as medical consenus assumes.</p>
<p>This tallys with the compelling field of epigenetics, which examines changes in our DNA (nature) brought about by environment (nurture). These can be passed down the generations, and include traumatic experiences. This was proven by <a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24029109/" target="_blank">landmark surveys</a> using data assembled from descendants of Holocaust survivors. Of course it also works for colonialism, industrialised warfare, poverty, patriarchy and lovelessness. It’s a new and complex but far from fringe area: my mother is an identical twin who has been taking part in NHS <a href="https://www.guysandstthomas.nhs.uk/research/studies/twins.aspx" target="_blank">epigentic research</a> funded by good old Wellcome Trust for decades.</p>

<h2>‘The Eagle and the Condor’ is a two thousand year-old propehcy that could be said to predict the colonisation of the Americas, the resulting cultural holocaust, and a re-emergence of mystical healing techniques. </h2>
<p>The peoples of the Condor – indigeneous Americans – and the Eagle, western colonisers – will finally come together ushering a new paradigm of enlightenment. </p>
<p>Columbus’ First Voyage landed in the Carribbean in 1492. Syncronicity fans note that in 1994 Terrence McKenna published <em>Food of the Gods</em> and Dr Allan Schore released <em>Affect Regulation and the Origin of the Self.</em></p>
<p>The aforementioned Shuar shaman John Perkins has alluded to the propehcy as prompting a shift from the West’s ‘Death Economy’ based on competiting over limited resources to a ‘Life Economy’ where wellbeing is paramount (in our abundant era, the Death Economy is arguably so past its due that its basis in scarcity has even had to be simulated by, for example, western governments implementing policies to artificially raise house prices).</p>
<p>This prophecy is not uncomparable to Western astrology’s Age of Aquarius (which both Carl Jung in <em>Aion</em> and Aleister Crowley with his ‘Age of Horus’ suggested will have a non-dual flipside, but that is for another day).</p>
<p>The prophecy offers the very seductive idea of ‘Pachakuti’ a time of reconciliation and healing. But is it real?</p>
<p>“Who knows what’s real?” says Dr Tafur, when talking about ‘entity’ encounters’ and other sublime ingredients of the mystic experience, “we’re dealing with a mystery and we have to discern. Be careful with anything, no matter what, because things can be tricky in those spaces. The medicine itself is not trying to trick us. If there is light, and positive healing spirit, it’s clear. But if there’s any doubt, there’s no doubt – just wait. And don’t worry that you’re missing out, because you’re learning what could be good for you and what isn’t.”</p>
<p>It is your journey, and there are no clear answers. “Respecting your space is important, and these things should respect you too,” he says, “but this isn’t the sort of thing that can be learned on the internet. It’s messy and there’s room for projection and confusion.” Enforce boundaries as you should outside of DMT hyperspace.</p>
<p>“Hearing ‘is it real?’ from the other end gets kind of boring after a while,” says Dr Tafur. </p>
<p>Whether ‘entities’, or prophecies, or indeed ‘limbic resonance’ are facts is to miss the point.</p>
<p>Instead, ask – what are the feelings? And are they benefitting us?</p>

<p>Despite what the <a href="https://www.youtube.com/c/gotmilkblood" target="_blank">sort of thing</a> I write in my day job would have you believe, government-backed public education openly invests in paranormal research (for better or worse). It even issues press releases about the positive findings. Here in the UK Northampton University is the crucible of not-so-forbidden learning, with parapsychological research taking place across several departments. </p>
<p>Published in 2015 by the notoriously conservative American Psychological Association <em>Transcendent Mind</em> contains an enthusiastic review of official investigations into consciousness, the soul, supra-physical existence and the cosmic whole. </p>
<p>Drs <a href="https://www.mossbridgeinstitute.com/news/" target="_blank">Julia Mossbridge</a> and <a href="https://baruss.ca/books.htm" target="_blank">Imants Baruss</a>, a former engineering scholar who quit to work as a roofer before taking up psychology, are heavy-hitters in the field.
