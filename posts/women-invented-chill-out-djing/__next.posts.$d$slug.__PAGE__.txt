<div class="sqs-html-content" data-sqsp-text-block-content>
  <p class="" >When 50s LSD researcher Abram Hoffer’s wife Rose soothed a tripping patient by changing a jarring record to an elegant Bach number, she was henceforth delegated I/C the tunes.&nbsp;</p><p class="" >Testing music for effectiveness began in 1956 under <a href="https://chacruna.net/how-music-therapists-helped-build-psychedelic-therapy/" target="_blank">Hermina E Browne</a>, director of music therapy at New Jersey State Hospital during psychedelic sessions for alcoholics. Browne’s major innovations were to divide the soundtrack into five thirty-minute parts plus – I love this one – <em>put the music on for half an hour, then turn it off again for the same time</em>. </p><p class="" >Browne’s playlist categories were:</p><p class="" >1 Relaxing to tense</p><p class="" >2 Very tense, disturbed with a purpose</p><p class="" >3 Solemn, meditative, self-searching, spiritual</p><p class="" >4 Relaxing, spiritual</p><p class="" >5 Reconciling, restoration of confidence, feeling of hope and faith</p><p class="" >Browne passed her findings on to E Thayer Gaston, ‘The Father of Music Therapy’ who eschewed her policy of ‘Five Moods Projected’. Instead he insisted on music ‘familiar’ to the patient. (The Fadiman <a href="https://www.psychedelicexplorersguide.com" target="_blank">handbook</a> suggests the first time voyagers ask to change the music, stand your ground gently, then cave in if they ask again).</p><h2 >Hermina Browne’s innovation was to put the music on for half an hour, then turn it off again for another thirty minutes</h2><p class="" >Later during the 1970s <a href="https://chacruna.net/how-music-therapists-helped-build-psychedelic-therapy/">Helen Bonny</a> considered music&nbsp;‘intersubjectively verifiable’ and set out to prove it. Her Maryland Hospital is noted for using <em>four</em> therapists in psychedelic sessions, always including a music specialist.</p><p class="" >Bonny’s unpublished research demonstrated certain universal qualities, but she’s even better known for conceiving <a href="https://voices.no/index.php/voices/article/view/1876/1640" target="_blank">Guided Imagery and Music</a>, the leading form of music therapy in the present day. </p><p class="" >The soundtrack curated by women for early LSD test remains the template for chill out mixes – plus Johns Hopkins University (<a href="https://www.hopkinsmedicine.org/news/articles/inside-the-johns-hopkins-psilocybin-playlist">its seven-hour playlist here</a>) and MAPS therapy sessions – today.</p><p class="" >Unless you prefer the 2020’s foremost psychedelic tunestress <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FKKIZ9O6tCA">DJ Bus Replacement Service</a>.</p>
