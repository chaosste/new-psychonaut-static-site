<h2>My bookshelf weighs a ton</h2>
<h4>Notable new purchases for the occult library. It’s supposed to be strictly second hand snap-ups only. But I’m flagging on that to be honest. It’s now more in the <em>spirit</em> of a second hand bookstore. This week: <em>The Entropy of Bones </em>by Ayize Jama-Everett</h4>
</div>
<p>       <figure style="width: 1200px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="https://www.abebooks.co.uk/9780992808884/Science-Microdosing-Psychedelics-Passie-Torsten-099280888X/plp"><img decoding="async" src="https://newpsychonaut.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/bones-book.avif?format=original" alt="     You’ll probably have to get it from/in America or off of Amazon   "></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">You’ll probably have to get it from/in America or off of Amazon</figcaption></figure> </p>
<div class="sqs-html-content" data-sqsp-text-block-content>
<p><a href="https://ayizejamaeverett.com">Ayize Jama-Everett</a> handled the psychedelic racial awareness training on Vital. There’s a bit of a syncronicity here because I came across his name before in relation to my martial arts side hustle <a href="https://www.battlesoflondon.com">Battles of London</a> (‘The brand making fight clothing cool’ says <em>Men’s Health</em>). I was going to hassle him for a short story for our print mag. But, y’know, lockdown.</p>
<p>Reading the signs, I bought this one because it had a snake on it.</p>
<p>The cover’s actually by <a href="https://www.johnjenningsstudio.com">John Jennings</a>, a top-flight comic artist (and more) who Jama-Everett’s created a graphic novel with. Actually, one of the things I admire about <a href="https://www.amazon.co.uk/Entropy-Bones-Ayize-Jama-Everett/dp/1618731033/ref=tmm_pap_swatch_0?_encoding=UTF8&amp;qid=&amp;sr="><em>The Entropy of Bones</em></a> is that Jama-Everett must get told all the time ‘Why isn’t it a graphic novel?’ and that he’s nonethless written two others in the same vein (series, in fact).</p>
<p>Anyway, what’s really good about The Entropy of Bones is it’s about getting into your body. Which is admittedly also in danger of bnecoming psycedelic rhetoric, one of the unofficial themes of this <em>Unofficial Vital Student Zine</em>. </p>
<p>Plus there’s lots of other five star biz too, like: super-powered martial arts, smokeable psychedelic fungi, international-level decadence, weed farming and jungle drum ‘n’ bass.</p>
