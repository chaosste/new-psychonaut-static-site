<p>       <figure style="width: 1200px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" src="https://newpsychonaut.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/bacteria-psychedelic-ben-jacob-art2-Enhanced.avif?format=original" alt="   Bacteria operating in fracal patterns observed by the late Dr Eschel Ben-Jacob   "><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Bacteria operating in fracal patterns observed by the late Dr Eschel Ben-Jacob</figcaption></figure> </p>
<div class="sqs-html-content" data-sqsp-text-block-content>
<p><em>Pochonia Chlamydosporia</em> is a fungus recently discovered to <a href="https://parasitesandvectors.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/s13071-020-04402-w">use ketamine</a> for flushing parasites out of its host plant’s roots.</p>
<p>Discovered and hyped only recently, there are two eyebrow-raising elements to this. </p>
<p>Firstly ketamine is a ‘designer drug’, or as elite space commando and neuroscientist <a href="https://www.buildingalienworlds.com/">Andrew Gallimore</a> wrote on <a href="https://twitter.com/alieninsect/status/1514393509519757313">Twitter</a>, “a perfect example of a purely synthetic molecule. The arylcyclohexylamines, of which ketamine is the prototypic example, are a product entirely of the human mind… Oh wait…” </p>
<p>Did the fungus get hold of it via the mycelial network? Is biology categorisable to the extent that a substance arrived at in lab tests can be naturally occurring but not yet discovered? Or did the <a href="https://www.amazon.co.uk/Reality-Switch-Technologies-Psychedelics-Exploration/dp/1739110102/ref=sr_1_1?crid=2DB0XGB2M77PZ&amp;keywords=andrew%20gallimore&amp;qid=1662385939&amp;sprefix=andrew%20gallimore%2Caps%2C58&amp;sr=8-1">Reality Switch Technologies</a> Gallimore writes about come into play, somehow?</p>
</div>
<p>       <figure style="width: 1024px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" src="https://newpsychonaut.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Mode-of-action-of-fungal-parasites-of-nematode-eggs-a-Field-emission-scanning-electron-Enhanced-1.avif?format=original" alt="   Some ketamine killing parasitic worm eggs   "><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Some ketamine killing parasitic worm eggs</figcaption></figure> </p>
<div class="sqs-html-content" data-sqsp-text-block-content>
<p>Secondly: <em>Pochonia Chlamydosporia </em>uses ketamine as an anti-microbial agent. A <a href="https://psychedelicreview.com/exploring-ketamines-antimicrobial-effects-fungal-sources-part-1/">robust two-parter</a> in <em>Psychedelic Science Review</em> dug out a 1987 study where ketamine did a great job killing bacterial heart lining infections in rabbits – and stopped the rabies virus breeding in rats. </p>
<p>In 2002 ketamine was tested against market antibiotics and crushed <em>Staphylococcus aureus, </em>a common opportunistic bacteria that causes all kinds of nastiness, from pimples and impetigo to MRSA and pneumonia… and has become 80% resistant to antibiotics since penicillin was invented in 1943. </p>
<p>The 2020 report from the un-putdownable <a href="https://parasitesandvectors.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/s13071-020-04402-w"><em>Parasites &amp; Vectors</em></a><em> </em>journal<em> </em>tested ketamine against agricultural anti-wormer ivermectin, which used to be advertised on TV back in the West Country to give you an idea of how widespread its farming use is. Ketamine performed equally well.</p>
<p>But what has actually captured the imaginations of many in the space is that this means ketamine counts as a ‘plant medicine’. I eagerly await the complex mythologies, concept albums, groundbreaking scientific discoveries, artistic genres, philosophical insights, and <a href="https://chacruna.net/gift-spider-woman-spinning-weaving-womanhood-among-matsigenka-peru/">bespoke geometric fabric designs</a> that shall surely now emerge from the ketamine subculture, such as it currently is.</p>
