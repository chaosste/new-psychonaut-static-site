<h2>My bookshelf weighs a ton</h2>
<h4>Notable new purchases for the occult library. Strictly second hand snap-ups only. This week: <em>The Archaic Revival</em> by Terrence McKenna. </h4>
</div>
<figure style="width: 1200px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" src="https://newpsychonaut.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/strange-ecs.avif?format=original" alt="   £25 usually £100+   "><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">£25 usually £100+</figcaption></figure>
<div class="sqs-html-content" data-sqsp-text-block-content>
<p>Stories of space narcotics by major authors collected by druid of derring-do Michael Parry (d. 2014) , including <em>The New Accelerator</em> by H G Wells, <em>Subjectivity by Norman Spinrad</em> and <em>What to Do Until the Analyst Comes</em> by Frederik Pohl. </p>
<p>Plus a superb cover naturally.</p>
<p>If you like this sort of thing and haven’t read <em>The Employees</em>, a 2020 Booker Prize nominee by Danish author Olga Ravn, a ‘disconcertingly quotidian space opera’ (<a href="https://www.theguardian.com/books/2021/may/12/the-employees-a-workplace-novel-of-the-22nd-century-by-olga-ravn-review-am-i-human"><em>The Guardian</em></a>), do so at light speed.</p>
<p>I can’t look at books like this without being hauntd by a sci fi short story about a co-ed college on a space station where the boys get these pets called teasels. I couldn’t find any reference to it online which was chilling in itself.</p>
