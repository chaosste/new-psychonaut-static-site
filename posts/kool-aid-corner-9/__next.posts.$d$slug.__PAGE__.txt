<h4>Notable new purchases for the occult library. Strictly second hand snap-ups only. This week: <em>Storm of Steel</em> by Ernst Junger</h4>
<p data-rte-preserve-empty="true">
</p></div>
<p>       <figure style="width: 1200px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" src="https://newpsychonaut.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/junger-steel.avif?format=original" alt="   Another score from the Brick Lane bookstore, £6.50   "><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Another score from the Brick Lane bookstore, £6.50</figcaption></figure> </p>
<div class="sqs-html-content" data-sqsp-text-block-content>
<p>“No other writer has thus opened my eyes,” wrote Albert Hoffman of Junger’s sublime nature writing in <em>LSD: My Problem Child</em>. Although the psychedelic inventor who corresponded with Junger after WW2, was keen to point out that he was less into Junger’s ‘earlier books’ about ‘war and a new type of human being.’</p>
<p>Junger was Germany’s greatest military hero of WW1. Throughout his career he consistently refused to apologise for  embracing conflict when necessary, perhaps in contrast to his proto-hippy views that influnced the 60s counter-culture. He did accept that the warrior was powerless against the march of tech. “In war you learn your lessons, and they stay learned, but the tuition fees are high” is just one of the gems from this terrifying and exhilarating account of trench warfare that’s often uncomfortably, but necessarily, voyueristic. </p>
