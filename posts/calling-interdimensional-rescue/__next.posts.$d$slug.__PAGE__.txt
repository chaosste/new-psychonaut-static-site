<h3><span class="sqsrte-text-color--custom" style="color: #498DDA">Crisis management is the reality of integration therapy, professionals say</span></h3>
</div></div></div>
<p>       <figure style="width: 1200px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="https://www.eriskayconnection.com/home/130-transcendent-country-of-the-mind.html"><img decoding="async" src="https://newpsychonaut.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Dimension-3-from-the-series-Transcendent-Country-of-the-Mind-Enhanced.avif?format=original" alt="    From Transcendent Country of the Mind by Sari Soninen published by      The Eriskay Collection    "></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">From Transcendent Country of the Mind by Sari Soninen published by      The Eriskay Collection</figcaption></figure> </p>
<div class="sqs-html-content" data-sqsp-text-block-content>
<h2>Lisbon-based psychotherapist <a href="https://www.marcaixala.com/en/">Marc Aixala</a> says the reality of psychedelic integration is most clients come seeking help, having a bad time of some description. </h2>
<p>“So my expertise is more focused on people in difficulties,” Aixala <a href="https://psychedelicstoday.com/2022/11/22/pt374/">told Vital lecturer Kyle Buller on the Psychedelics Today podcast</a>: “When I talk about integration, that’s what I’m referring to the most.”</p>
<p><a href="https://www.marcaixala.com/en/">Aixala</a> crunched the data on years of psychedelic damage limitation. The most common issues are presented in his book <a href="https://blackwells.co.uk/bookshop/product/Psychedelic-Integration-by-Marc-B-Aixal/9780907791393"><em>Psychedelic Integration</em></a> out now.</p>
<h2>“Psychedelics teach us that the greatest healer is in ourselves”</h2>
<p>Among the top comedown crises: anxiety and insomnia obviously; struggling to process what emerged from the experience; inner conflict with an instruction supposedly delivered from on high; actual flashbacks and HPPD; reality breakdown; plus good old edgy, lingering discomfort.</p>
<h2>“Don’t create a need for integration in people that don’t have it”</h2>
<p>Apportioning blame, Aixala’s figures cite lack of suitable preparation, exacerbation of existing mental health issues, dodgy healers, and what Aixala calls “Repeated intakes without integration, a new trend in which people go from experience to experience. They for instance care more about transpersonal entities, losing reality in quite a severe way.” </p>
<p>Of depth work, journaling, downloading-but-never-using meditation apps, and the other trappings of vanilla, middle class ‘integration’ Aixala says: “Most of the time we can do that on our own.” </p>
<p>The former telephone engineer adds, “I’m careful as a therapist not to create an additional need for integration in people that don’t have it. One of the things that psychedelics teaches us, is that greatest healer is in ourselves.”</p>
