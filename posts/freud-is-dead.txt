<h2>Freud is Dead. And we have killed him.</h2>
<p>My loose understanding of the gossip in the ivory towers of psychology is that Freudian psychoanalysis maintains an iron grip on legitimacy. </p>
<p>This seems to have crumbled almost overnight like empires do. Psychoanalysis’ spiritual home <a href="https://tavistockandportman.nhs.uk">The Tavistock Clinic</a> has been <a href="https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-62335665">rocked by scandal</a>. And Imperial College didn’t use psychoanalysis as such in PsiloDep 2. Because it’s <a href="https://newpsychonaut.com/insights/vital-student-zine-week-five/transpersonal-psychology">only been an initial part of psychedelic therapy</a> as documented by Stanislav Grof. </p>
<p>Indeed Grof’s former colleague, TV’s <a href="https://newpsychonaut.com/insights/vital-student-zine-week-four/the-metaphysician-will-see-you-now-39nt2">Dr Bill Richards</a> who’s still kicking it himself at John Hopkins (and on Netflix) advised on Acceptance, Connection and Embodiment (ACE) therapy, the model applied by Imperial College in its landmark trials testing psilocybin against a market SSRI anti-depressant.</p>
<p>“The trials followed a standard psychedelic psychedelic therapy format: preparation, the high dose, and then integration alongside an Acceptance and Commitment Therapy [<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Association_for_Behavioral_and_Cognitive_Therapies">ACT</a>] adapted model,” relays Ashleigh.  </p>
<p>ACT is a kind-of proactive mindfulness to encourage ‘psychological flexibility’ an adaptive mindset resilient to stressful events. The psychological flexibility model or PFM is referred to in the title of Dr Ros’ stealth bomber of a paper, <a href="https://www.researchgate.net/publication/344648056_The_use_of_the_psychological_flexibility_model_to_support_psychedelic_assisted_therapy"><em>The use of the psychological flexibility model to support psychedelic assisted therapy</em></a><em> </em>which points out the approach is in use in trials at NYU and Yale, too.</p>
<p>And guess what? Everyone prefers it to being told stuff in the past they were doing their best to forget has ruined both their present and future, so it’s going to cost them £200 a week. Plus, ACT’s explanatory infographic is a <em>freaking icosahedron</em>, the sacred geometric form that’s like a 20-sided <em>Dungeons &amp; Dragons</em> dice.</p>
</div>
<p>       <figure style="width: 850px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" src="https://newpsychonaut.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Dimensions-of-Psychological-Flexibility-Model-Note-This-figure-demonstrates-the-six.avif?format=original" alt="   Acceptance and commitment therapy’s ‘hexaflex’ graph   "><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Acceptance and commitment therapy’s ‘hexaflex’ graph</figcaption></figure> </p>
<div class="sqs-html-content" data-sqsp-text-block-content>
<p>ACT is empirically proven in all of these tests we’re beginning to think will be endless, and approved by EG the NHS. </p>
<h2>“It’s a very complex experience that people are going through. So we’re using lots of different influences”</h2>
<p>But because ACT’s not Freudian analysis, it gets crap from the old guard. Seems like nobody cares what they think any more, though, because their way hasn’t worked, except for them. And they gave kids gender reassignment.</p>
<p>That’s not all. “As clinicians, we were drawing on a lot of different psychological theories to support people because it’s a very complex experience that people are going through. So we’re using lots of different influences,” says Ashleigh.</p>
<p>ACE is Dr Rosalind ‘Ros’ Watts’ remix of ACT. It even has P-ACE (for preparation) and I-ACE (for integration). It includes aspects of polyvagal theory, lived experience and non-dual thinking to name but two. </p>
<p>And there’s even deferral to the inner healer or ‘homeostatic instinct’ to give it its new scientific name in ACE. Metaphors employed for the healing process include ‘<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?list=LLkr7fKkIPPkrm9SosIBFN4w&amp;v=2KKrZQ3m77U">diving for pearls</a>’ illustrated by specially prepared visualisers. Plus, let’s not forget, a new ambient John Hopkins’ LP <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?index=7&amp;list=PLHkFG2EjLJZGlK-gH7hnOXvjzcOrezG9w&amp;v=YhRybYyrNaI">bespoke-made for the trials</a>.</p>
<h2>“We’re asking people to open up to emotional pain at a pace they may never have experienced”</h2>
<p>Stan Grof says psychoanalysis was useful for the earliest stages of treating in-patients, but soon gave way to even more fundamental realms of the psyche – and body – that required knowledge not only of cutting-edge thinkers. </p>
<p>“I wonder how possible it is to grasp”</p>
<p>He names William Reich for <em>perinatal matrix III</em> when the body spasms start (not seen many of those on the <a href="https://www.vitalpsychedelictraining.com">course</a> yet) and Jung in his <em>Red Book</em> days, but the ‘<a href="https://newpsychonaut.com/insights/vital-student-zine-week-five/transpersonal-psychology">transpersonal</a>’ (IE weird), plus theology, literature and philosophy too. </p>
<p>“We invite them to tap into a sense that there may be wisdom and guidance to be learned from emotional pain, and difficult experiences in life,” says Ashleigh. There’s practical considerations that don’t come up in that room your therapist has in Finsbury Park with the knitted throws and knackered dreamcatcher on the ceiling. </p>
<p>“We’re asking people to open up to emotional pain, to an extent, and at a pace they may never have experienced before,” says Ashleigh, “I wonder how possible it is to grasp. We wrangled over what we tell people beforehand, so they can make an ethical and informed decision about taking part in a treatment like this.”</p>
<p>‘Must we ourselves become gods simply to appear worthy of it?’ opined Nietzsche upon his most famous line, ‘God is dead.’ The firebrand philosopher meant a sense of shared, guiding ideology rather than the monotheistic biblical concept of God. </p>
<p>He was mostly right, because us stupid normies did come up with a new God – science. The bits of that which considered our relationships to each other, so the only ones that counted, originated from Sigmund Freud, and <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8QxRUM6T2aY">Richard Dawkins</a> via Charles Darwin. </p>
<p>The new anti-religion preached a mirror image of historical spirituality: humans were essentially chimpanzees, except cleverer, so even more unpleasant to each other. </p>
<p>Arts, achievement, compassion, shared laughter… all just tactics in the game to get ahead. Beneath it all we were just throwing our turds at each other, and pretending not to enjoy getting screwed by the alpha male. </p>
<p>Who hasn’t been seduced by this perverse science at some point? Especially on cocaine, like Sigmund Freud was half the time. </p>
<p>Eventually though it gets… depressing.</p>
