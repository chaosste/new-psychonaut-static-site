<h2>My bookshelf weighs a ton</h2>
<h4>Notable new purchases for the occult library. Strictly second hand snap-ups only. This week: <em>LSD Psychotherapy by Stanislav Grof</em></h4>
</div>
<p>       <figure style="width: 1200px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="https://www.wob.com/en-gb/books/matthew-oram-university-of-cal/trials-of-psychedelic-therapy/9781421426204#NPB9781421426204"><img decoding="async" src="https://newpsychonaut.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/grof-psych.avif?format=original" alt=" Buy MAPS’ natty two-volume   Way of the Psychonaut   set "></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Buy MAPS’ natty two-volume   Way of the Psychonaut   set</figcaption></figure> </p>
<div class="sqs-html-content" data-sqsp-text-block-content>
<p>If James Fadiman’s Psdychedelic Explorer’s Guide is the basic primer on Vital, this is the advanced text. </p>
<p>I was saving up for MAPS’ natty two-volume <a href="https://maps.org/product/the-way-of-the-psychonaut-two-volume-set/"><em>Way of the Psychonaut</em></a> (WotP) set but so many clever people mentioned <em>LSD Psychotherapy</em> off-hand in their lectures, and implored students to read it, that I followed the signs and grabbed a copy of the general 1980 release. I wasn’t disappointed.</p>
<p>Within Grof’s reflections were the missing four corners of the jigsaw puzzle: LSD Therapy squared my impressions of the experience, its mystic and medicinal qualities, plus even putting on the the blindfold and ‘going under’. </p>
<p>LSD Therapy features illustrated plates picturing art created on LSD by Grof’s patients, including a chap who, while others are painting tableaux featuring EG a rising phoenix, daubs cartoons about his wife and his car.  </p>
<p>I get the impression Grof books are like Smiths albums and crossover a little content-wise, so buy WotP rather than scurry around Abe Books snapping up what you can.</p>
