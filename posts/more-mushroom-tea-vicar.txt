<h2><span class="sqsrte-text-color--custom" style="color: #8A877D"><strong>Savvy brits in the space are sussed to self-care. But the vulnerable are left behind</strong></span></h2>
</div></div></div>
<p>       <figure style="width: 1500px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" src="https://newpsychonaut.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/graf-brick-lane-2.avif?format=original" alt="   Contemporary graffiti in east London   "><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Contemporary graffiti in east London</figcaption></figure> </p>
<div class="sqs-html-content" data-sqsp-text-block-content>
<h2>Here’s a ray of optimism, before I start even attempting to unravel the respective messes that are Britain’s drug laws and mental health provision.</h2>
<p>A judge in Cumbria, northern England just <a href="https://www.newsandstar.co.uk/news/20271560.eden-mans-magic-mushroom-case-linked-depression-research/">said she hoped ’the law will catch up with science’</a> when pardoning an accused man for growing his own magic mushrooms to benefit his mental health.</p>
<p>Britain has the highest depression rate among children in Europe, along with one-third of the continent’s drug overdose deaths and its worst alcohol problem. Mental health problems cost the British economy £118 billion annually. The situation is <a href="https://unherd.com/2022/08/inside-britains-psychiatric-nightmare/">apparently more dismal than we even think</a>. Lockdown saw a <a href="https://thecommissiononyounglives.co.uk/commission-on-young-lives-warns-post-covid-childrens-mental-health-services-are-buckling-under-pressure-and-putting-vulnerable-young-people-at-greater-risk-of-exploitation-serious-violence-and-abus/">47% increase in young people seeking help</a> and I need hardly quote again my recent article elsewhere detailing the stigma that still exists in the workplace around stress and burnout.</p>
<p>It’s characteristic of the British legislature to turn a benign blind eye to self-medication while dragging its feet on psilocybin prescriptions. Former prime minister (PM) Boris Johnson and his pantomime villain advisor Dominic Cummings supposedly had psychedelic therapy as a <a href="https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-58980382">political cause celébre</a><em> </em>partly because Brexit meant chances to the law could be actioned quicker. Now they’re out of the game, things are even worse in the corridors of power. </p>
<h2>Unlikely Men in Tights of this particular pantomime are the <a href="https://www.cdprg.co.uk/">Conservative Drug Policy Reform Group</a> </h2>
<p>UK home secretary Priti Patel says she’ll ban ‘middle class’ cannabis smokers from nightclubs and take away their passports to <a href="https://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-11022957/Priti-Patel-targets-cannabis-users-plans-confiscate-passports.html">derision from even Daily Mail readers</a>. Front runner for new PM Liz Truss has <a href="https://www.mirror.co.uk/news/politics/liz-truss-cannabis-leaflet-days-27675938">turned Judas</a> on her 420-friendly past. </p>
<p>The centre left is no better with its leader Keir Starmer, a former head of public prosecutions, saying he’s “seen too much damage” in his former role. Dude, the unremittingly grim extraction economy lifestyle is the problem across all classes especially the estate-condemned non-working class. Not the weed itself. </p>
<p>While kids opting for dank oblivion above all else is a problem, it is hardly caused by marijuana alone and previous alternatives like booze and heroin are frankly worse. My entirely subjective opinion from the ground is that the approach reeks of not upsetting near-senile, control-freak baby-boomers.</p>
<p>Unlikely Men in Tights of this particular pantomime are the <a href="https://www.cdprg.co.uk/">Conservative Drug Policy Reform Group</a> that are actually part of the UK’s centre-right Conservative [Tory] Party. Its campaign to legalise cannabis and psychedelic therapies has the blessing of former prime minister John Major, ex-Tory leader William Hague, current Northamptonshire police, fire and crime commissioner Stephen Mold, plus ex-MI5 (it’s like Homeland Security) chiefs Lord Evans and Baroness Eliza Manningham-Butler.</p>
<p>Over half of voters from even right-wing parties believe in the legalisation of psychedelic therapy, according to a YouGov poll quoted by broadcaster and former advisor to PM Theresa May <a href="https://www.theneweuropean.co.uk/the-government-opposition-to-psychedelic-therapies-is-damaging/">Tom Swarbrick</a>. Thought leaders like the redoubtable <a href="https://www.thenation.com/article/society/legal-drugs-psychedelics-corporate/">Zoe Cormier</a> of good eggs <a href="https://guerillascience.org/">Guerrilla Science</a> are also in the media front lines doing the mushroom god’s work.</p>
<p>Meanwhile the country’s largest NHS trust are opening a new dedicated facility in the grounds of the former ‘Bedlam’ hospital alongside Compass Pathways which you can <a href="https://newpsychonaut.com/vital-zine-9/maudsley-nhs-compass-bethlehem-hospital">read about elsewhere in this issue</a>.</p>
<p>The naturally British reaction is to quietly do what it seems the justice system, NHS and general public are already doing. Which is plough on regardless leaving the government apparatus and armchair windbags to their own ineffectual posturing. </p>
