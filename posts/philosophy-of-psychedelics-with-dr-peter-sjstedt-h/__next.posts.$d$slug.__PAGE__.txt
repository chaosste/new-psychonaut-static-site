<h4><strong>My unofficial <em>Vital Study Zine </em>#6 with observations from Vital Psychedelic Training and recent happenings in the space</strong></h4>
</div>
<p>       <figure style="width: 809px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><a href="https://www.charlottewendylaw.net/Film.html"><img decoding="async" src="https://newpsychonaut.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Intertice-Eating-the-Weather02.avif?format=original" alt="      From Internice Eating the Weather by Charlotte Wendy Law   "></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">From Internice Eating the Weather by Charlotte Wendy Law</figcaption></figure> </p>
<div class="sqs-html-content" data-sqsp-text-block-content>
<h3>“The most exciting talk is the space right now is coming from philosophers,” said the 21st Century’s answer to David Attenborough, Merlin Sheldrake at June’s Breaking Convention conference in London. </h3>
<p>Psychedelic philosophy or ‘psy-phi’ aims to legitimise psychedelic insight, the culture of consciousness expansion and post-industrial ideology.</p>
<p>The discipline applies academic rigour to tripped-out insight. It suggests how expanded consciousness can be applied in areas ranging from business to ecology – and revamp psychotherapy.</p>
<p>Fittingly, psychedelic philosophy’s origin is the <a href="https://philosophyofpsychedelics.com">West Country of England</a>. The academic centre being Exeter University, which announced the first bachelor’s degree in psychedelic studies at its 2022 <a href="https://blogs.exeter.ac.uk/philosophyandpsychedelics/transdisciplinary-research-colloquium-on-psychedelics/">Philosophy of Psychedelics</a> conference. </p>
<p>Other cosmopolitan hubs like Amsterdam and Berlin have got their eyes on our special cider. Exeter University’s much-anticipated compilation <a href="https://www.bloomsbury.com/uk/philosophy-and-psychedelics-9781350231610/"><em>Philosophy and Psychedelics: Frameworks for Exceptional Experience</em></a>, is published by Bloomsbury this summer. And its colourful co-editor Dr Peter Sjöstedt-Hughes materialised before Vital students this week, direct from Dartmoor.</p>
<p>Dr Sjöstedt-Hughes, who edited Exeter’s tome, is a leading light in the movement. The reboot of Marvel superhero Karnak is modelled on him. Beyond providing much needed aesthetic airs – he is rarely photographed without a falcon on his arm – he provides psychedelic philosophy’s imaginative spark. </p>
<p>“Humphry Osmond wrote to Huxley that he believed LSD’s greatest potential was its philosophical, social and religious implications,” he says, “I think the potential is still there.”</p>
<p>Dr Sjöstedt-Hughes has documented the rich relationship between philosophy and psychedelic use, and spearheaded the rehabilitation of controversial philosophers including Henri Bergson, Ernst Junger and Nietzsche alongside rebranding cuddlier thinkers like Spinoza, George Bernard Shaw and Sir Humphry Davy. </p>

Find him at <a href="https://philosopher.eu">philosopher.eu</a> and <a href="https://mobile.twitter.com/PeterSjostedtH">@PeterSjostedtH</a>.</p>
